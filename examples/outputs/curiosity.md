STORY:
Lily skipped out into her backyard, her rain boots splashing in the leftover puddles. The sun peeked through the clouds, casting a warm glow over the wet grass and glistening leaves.

As she twirled around, Lily looked up at the sky and asked, "Grandma, why does the rain fall from the sky?"

Her grandmother, who was tending to her garden nearby, smiled and beckoned Lily over. "Come sit with me, dear," she said, patting the soft earth next to her.

Lily settled down, the damp soil cool beneath her. Her grandmother took Lily's tiny hand in hers and began to tell a story. "Long, long ago, the sky was filled with fluffy white clouds, just like big cotton balls. These clouds carried tiny droplets of water, like little gifts from above."

"The clouds traveled far and wide, looking for places to share their gifts. When they found a patch of dry earth, they would gently release the droplets, like a soft, misty hug."

Lily's eyes widened in wonder as she imagined the clouds floating across the vast sky, sprinkling raindrops below.

Her grandmother continued, "And so, the rain falls to water the earth, to help the flowers grow, and to bring us joy with its soothing melody."

Lily listened intently, feeling the magic of the rain in a whole new way. She hugged her grandmother tightly and whispered, "Thank you for telling me the story, Grandma."

As the day turned into evening, the sky darkened, and the first raindrops began to fall again. Lily snuggled into bed, the sound of rain tapping gently on her window lulling her into a peaceful sleep.

And as she drifted off, she knew that the rain was simply nature's way of giving love to the world.
