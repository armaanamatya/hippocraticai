## Story

OUTLINE:
Setup: In a cozy meadow, a tired bunny searches for a soft place to rest.
Small problem: The bunny can't find a spot that feels just right.
Kind resolution: With the help of friendly animals, the bunny discovers the softest patch of clover.
Wind-down: The bunny snuggles into the clover, eyes closing in peaceful sleep.

STORY:
In a meadow where fireflies dance and dandelions sway, there was a tired little bunny with floppy ears and a fluffy tail. The sun was sinking low, painting the sky in hues of pink and gold, and the bunny knew it was time to find a place to rest.

But oh, as the bunny hopped from tuft to tuft, from rock to flower, each spot felt too lumpy or too prickly for its liking. "Oh, where, oh where can I find the softest place to rest my weary paws?" the bunny sighed softly to itself.

Just then, a gentle breeze carried the bunny's plea to the ears of a wise old owl perched in a nearby tree. "Hoo-hoo, dear bunny, come this way," the owl hooted kindly. And so, the bunny followed the sound of the owl's voice to a hidden corner of the meadow.

There, nestled beneath a weeping willow tree, was a group of friendly field mice, chirping and chittering in excitement. "Try this patch of clover, dear bunny," they squeaked in unison, nudging the bunny towards a soft, lush bed of clover leaves.

With a grateful twitch of its nose, the bunny hopped onto the clover patch, feeling the delicate leaves give way beneath its weight like a fluffy cloud. "Oh, thank you, dear friends," whispered the bunny to the mice, heart aglow with warmth.

As the stars began to twinkle in the velvety sky above, the tired bunny curled up on the soft clover bed, eyes growing heavy with each gentle breath. The meadow creatures watched over the bunny, their soft lullabies weaving through the night air, cradling the bunny in a cozy blanket of peace.

And there, under the watchful gaze of the moon and the twinkling stars, the bunny drifted off into the sweetest, most restful slumber, dreams as soft as the petals of a rose. Goodnight, dear bunny, goodnight...
